All About Him



Early life and Education

Andrew David Huberman was born on September 26, 1975, in Palo Alto, California, to an Argentine physicist father who worked at Stanford University. He grew up in the Silicon Valley area, spending much of his youth playing sports like soccer and swimming, before becoming deeply immersed in the local skateboarding culture during his teenage years. Following his parents' divorce when he was 12, his focus on traditional schooling faltered, leading to a period of rebellion where he nearly dropped out of high school entirely. He turned things around after taking a temporary leave from formal education, finding a deep fascination with psychology and human behavior through his own experience in therapy. Re-engaging with his studies, he went on to earn a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology from the University of California, Santa Barbara in 1998, followed by a Master of Arts in Psychology from the University of California, Berkeley in 2000. He completed his formal academic training in 2004, earning a Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Neuroscience from the University of California, Davis.




His Career Life

Andrew Huberman’s professional career began with a postdoctoral research fellowship at Stanford University from 2006 to 2011, working under the renowned neurobiologist Ben Barres to develop genetic tools for mapping the visual system. He transitioned into academia as an Assistant Professor of Neurobiology and Neuroscience at the University of California, San Diego (UCSD) from 2011 to 2015, before returning to the Stanford University School of Medicine in 2016 as a tenured Associate Professor. At Stanford, he directs the Huberman Laboratory, which has published over 75 peer-reviewed papers in high-tier journals like Nature and Science, focusing on neural plasticity, stress mitigation, and using virtual reality (VR) to stimulate vision regeneration. Driven by a desire to make scientific research accessible during the COVID-19 pandemic, he co-founded Scicomm Media and launched the Huberman Lab podcast in January 2021. The show quickly grew into a massive global success, routinely ranking as a top health, science, and education podcast by translating dense laboratory findings into practical daily protocols for millions of listeners.
